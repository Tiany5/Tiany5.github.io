About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at Zhejiang University, advised by Prof. Wenguang Sun. I started my Ph.D. journey in 2022.

My research is dedicated to building trustworthy and powerful statistical methods for modern data analysis where classical assumptions often fail. My current work addresses a fundamental challenge in large-scale multiple testing: the unreliability of theoretical null distributions. To tackle this, I have developed a new framework that integrates the robustness of conformal inference with the power of empirical Bayes. This approach uses self-calibrated data to provide provable finite-sample FDR control without relying on restrictive distributional assumptions, ensuring more credible and powerful inferences in complex scientific settings.

Beyond static testing problems, I am also deeply interested in developing methodologies for sequential decision-making and online learning, where data arrives in a stream and decisions must be made in real-time.

Research Interests

My primary research interests include:

  • Conformal Inference & Distribution-Free Methods: Developing robust inference tools that provide valid statistical guarantees without strong distributional assumptions.
  • Empirical Bayes & Large-Scale Inference: Utilizing Empirical Bayes principles to borrow strength across all units, enhancing the power and reliability of inference in high-dimensional settings.
  • Online Multiple Testing & Sequential Analysis: Designing adaptive algorithms for error control in data streams and real-time decision-making scenarios.

Preprints & Publications

  • Conformalized Multiple Testing under Unknown Null Distribution with Symmetric Errors Yang Tian, Zinan Zhao & Wenguang Sun.
    Summary: This paper proposes SENS, a conformalized empirical Bayes method for large-scale multiple testing. SENS provides finite-sample FDR control without assuming a specific null distribution, relaxing the common Gaussian assumption to a weaker symmetry condition. It combines the power of empirical Bayes with the robustness of conformal inference.
