About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at Zhejiang University, advised by Prof. Wenguang Sun. I started my Ph.D. journey in 2022.

My research focuses on developing statistically rigorous and computationally efficient methods to address reliability challenges in modern data analysis. I am particularly interested in sequential decision-making under uncertainty and large-scale inference with unknown distributional properties. My work aims to provide strong theoretical guarantees (e.g., finite-sample error control) for high-stakes applications, such as real-time anomaly detection and large-scale scientific discovery.

Research Interests

My primary research interests include:

  • Conformal Inference & Distribution-Free Methods: Developing robust inference tools that provide valid statistical guarantees without strong distributional assumptions.
  • Online Multiple Testing & Sequential Analysis: Designing adaptive algorithms for error control in data streams, such as controlling the Missed Discovery Rate (MDR) in real-time settings.
  • Empirical Bayes & Large-Scale Inference: Leveraging data-driven techniques to enhance the power and reliability of multiple testing in high-dimensional problems.

Preprints & Publications

(* indicates equal contribution)

  • Sequential Anomaly Detection with Online Missed Discovery Rate Control Yang Tian, Wenguang Sun, Bowen Gang
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2509.04231
    Under the 2nd round of review at Journal of the American Statistical Association (JASA).
    Summary: This work introduces the first framework for online Missed Discovery Rate (MDR) control, crucial for safety-critical applications where missing an anomaly is costly. We propose an optimal test statistic (Lmdr) and a novel α-investing strategy to provide rigorous real-time guarantees in a semi-supervised setting.
    [arXiv]
  • Conformalized Multiple Testing under Unknown Null Distribution with Symmetric Errors Yang Tian, Wenguang Sun, et al.
    Preprint
    Submitted to Journal of the American Statistical Association (JASA).
    Summary: This paper proposes SENS, a conformalized empirical Bayes method for large-scale multiple testing. SENS provides finite-sample FDR control without assuming a specific null distribution, relaxing the common Gaussian assumption to a weaker symmetry condition. It combines the power of empirical Bayes with the robustness of conformal inference.
